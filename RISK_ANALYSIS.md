## ResidentsPath Risk Analysis

This shows the risk analysis conducted for this project.

| Risk Category | Specific Risk | Likelihood | Potential Impact | Impact Description | Mitigation Strategies |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Technical and operational | A resident completes the form, but submission fails before a case, ticket, or appointment is created. | Medium | High | the resident may have to repeat the process, and staff may never receive the request. | Validation is carried out before submission, errors are shown clearly to the user, and successful submissions generate a receipt with reference details. The draft saving feature also reduces the chance of a resident losing all progress part way through the form. |
| Technical and operational | Reference lookup, appointment cancellation, appointment check-in, or QR scanning fails because of browser, device, or connectivity problems. | Medium | Medium | residents or staff may be unable to complete what they're trying to do straight away. | The reference page supports both manual reference number entry and QR scanning, and the UI shows alerts when something goes wrong. If a resident facing page fails, staff can still manage queue and case state through staff pages. |
| Data security and privacy | A resident or other non-staff user tries to access staff-only pages or actions. | Low | High | this could expose staff information or allow unauthorised changes to queue or case state. | Staff routes are protected through role-based access controls, staff pages require the `Staff` Cognito group, and appointment check-in is limited to `Staff` and `HounslowHouseDevices` accounts. |
| Data security and privacy | Personal information is seen by the wrong person on a shared or public facing device. | Low to Medium | High | this would create a privacy risk for residents. | The form includes a privacy notice, resident facing pages only expose the minimum information needed, and case-management data is kept within staff only views rather than resident pages. |
| Accessibility and inclusion | A resident cannot complete the journey easily because they do not speak English, have trouble reading, or the UI is difficult to use on their device. | High | Medium | the resident may need help from reception staff or may abandon the system entirely. | The prototype includes multiple translations on supported pages, text-to-speech on selected pages, and support needs questions within the form. The form is also designed to be mobile friendly. |
| Ethical and safeguarding | A case that should be handled urgently or with extra care is not identified clearly enough for staff. | Medium | High | vulnerable residents or staff could be put at risk, or urgent cases may be dealt with too slowly. | Prioritisation is staff led rather than automated, staff can mark cases as priority and change queue positions, safeguarding flags can be applied, and the form records support needs and safe-to-contact information for staff to review. |
| User behaviour and queue integrity | Residents misunderstand estimated wait times and treat them as guarantees. | Medium | Medium | this may lead to frustration, complaints, or confusion when the queue changes. | Wait times are shown as a range rather than a single number, and the user dashboard highlights that urgent cases may still be prioritised ahead of others. |
| User behaviour and queue integrity | Residents lose their ticket or appointment details, miss notifications, or leave the building without understanding how to signal when they've returned. | Medium | Medium | the resident may miss their turn or need staff help to continue. | Submission receipts provide reference details for later use, the reference page supports follow-up lookup, and the user dashboard supports notifications as well as step-out and return. |
